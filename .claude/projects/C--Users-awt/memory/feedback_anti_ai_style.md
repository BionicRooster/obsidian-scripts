**File:** `D:\Obsidian\Main\01\Claude\2026-06-13 Anti-AI Style Guide.md`

**Why:** Derived from the Wikipedia "Signs of AI writing" field guide. Wayne asked for a style guide that turns these detection signals into actionable self-instructions.

**How to apply:**

1. **Banned words** — avoid pivotal, underscore, vibrant, tapestry, foster, garner, showcase, meticulous, robust, delve, align with, bolstered, enduring, crucial, key (as adjective), landscape (abstract), testament to, valuable. Use the plain word instead.

2. **Structural tics** — cut participial tails (-ing phrases appended to sentences), significance inflation ("marking a pivotal moment in..."), the "despite/however/challenges" formula, negative parallelism ("not just X, but Y"), rule of three triads, vague attribution ("experts argue"), "serves as" instead of "is," overloaded bold, title-case headings, canned conclusions ("In summary"), didactic disclaimers ("it's important to note").

3. **Use more of what AI avoids** — simple is/are/has, direct verbs (wrote not authored, moved not relocated, died not passed away), superlatives ("the first," "the only"), hedging qualifiers (very, perhaps, tends to).

4. **Self-check** — count -ing sentence endings, scan banned list, delete any sentence that could apply to a different subject, cut warm preambles and trailing offers to help, remove non-definitional bold.
